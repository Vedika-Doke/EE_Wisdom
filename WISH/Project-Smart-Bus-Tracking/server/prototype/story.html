1 · The bus

A small gadget rides along on every bus.

It has a GPS chip (so it knows where it is), a SIM card (so it can send a quick message), and it runs off the bus's own battery. Costs about ₹1,500 to build.

2 · The bus phones home

Every 25 seconds, it sends a tiny message.

Just a quick "I'm at this spot, going this fast." It goes over the same mobile network your phone uses. The whole day's data costs less than ₹2.

3 · The server figures it out

Where on the route is the bus, and when will it arrive?

The server keeps the shape of every route in memory. When a message lands, it finds the nearest spot on the route, then adds up how long the rest of the trip usually takes. That's the ETA.

4 · The depot can see everything

One screen for all the buses.

The MSRTC staff at the depot see every bus on a map, with how long until each arrives. If anything goes wrong — bus stuck, off route, signal lost — it shows up here right away.

5 · The passenger

Anyone can ask "where's my bus?" from any phone.

No smartphone needed, no app to download. The passenger calls a free number. A voice answers in their own language and tells them how many minutes until the bus arrives.

6 · When something goes wrong

If a bus diverts, everyone hears about it in seconds.

No more standing in the rain for an hour because the bus took a different road. Everyone who recently asked about that bus gets a text in their own language, automatically.
