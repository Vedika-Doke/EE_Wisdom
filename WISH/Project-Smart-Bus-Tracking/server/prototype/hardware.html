Slides 5 & 6 · Bread-board model + TI parts

The device that rides on every bus.

A small board with a GPS receiver, a 2G/GSM modem, and a TI microcontroller. Runs off the bus's own 12 V battery. Sends one 80-byte packet every 25 seconds. Bench-prototyped on a 2 × 4 breadboard with three TI ICs at its core.

Block-level schematic

Bus's 12 V hits the TI buck converter, drops to 5 V for the GSM modem (which needs the current), and gets cleaned further to 3.3 V by a TI LDO for the MCU and GPS receiver. The MSP430 reads GPS over one UART, pushes the packet over the other UART to the modem.

Bread-board photo mock-up

Bench prototype: MSP430FR5969 LaunchPad in the centre, NEO-6M GPS breakout top-right, SIM800L modem bottom-right, TI TPS54302 buck top-left, TI TPS7A0533 LDO bottom-left. Panic button + status LED on the right. Red = power, blue = serial signals, green/yellow = GPIO, black = ground.

Slide 6 — TI parts used

RolePartWhy this oneCost
MCU MSP430FR5969 Ultra-low power (~100 µA active, 0.4 µA in standby). FRAM survives power-cuts without data loss — critical for a vehicle that turns off every night. Two UARTs (one for GPS, one for modem). LaunchPad eval board makes prototyping painless. ₹420
Power-management IC TPS54302 3 A synchronous buck converter, 95% efficient. Drops 12 V bus battery to 5 V. Handles the ~2 A current bursts when the SIM800L transmits, which a cheap linear regulator would never survive. ₹160
LDO regulator TPS7A0533 Low-noise 3.3 V rail for the MCU and GPS. GPS-receiver sensitivity is killed by power-rail noise, so spending a few rupees on a clean LDO buys real position accuracy. ₹40
Analog front end (AFE) TLV9001 Rail-to-rail single-supply op-amp. Buffers the panic button signal (debounces mechanical bounce, prevents ESD from killing the MCU pin). ₹20
Wireless (optional) CC2640R2F BLE radio so a depot mechanic can walk up with a phone and read device logs without plugging in. Optional for v1 — included if BOM allows. ₹280
GPS receiver u-blox NEO-6M Industry-standard GPS module. TI doesn't make GPS, so we pair the TI MCU with this. Includes ceramic patch antenna or external SMA. ₹350
2G modem SIMCom SIM800L Cheap, well-documented, 2G coverage is universal in rural India even where 4G isn't. Talks to MCU over UART, accepts standard SIM. ₹250
Passives + connectors resistors, caps, headers 100 nF + 10 µF decoupling per IC, current-limit resistors, SMA pigtail, 2-pin power connector, fuse. ₹130
PCB / enclosure 2-layer 70×50 mm PCB + ABS box Tiny PCB once we move off the breadboard. ABS box screws under the bus dashboard, IP54-rated. ₹100

Why TI for this project

  • MSP430 family ranks among the lowest-power MCUs available — matters when the device is the only load draining the bus battery overnight.
  • TI's power-management portfolio (buck + LDO) lets us handle the SIM800L's 2 A burst without specialty parts.
  • Full development toolchain free: Code Composer Studio + LaunchPad eval boards. Important for a low-budget pilot.
  • India distribution: all parts available from Element14, Mouser-IN, Robu.in. No import lead times.

Wiring notes (for slide 5)

Power chain

12 V (bus battery) → 2 A blade fuse → TPS54302 VIN
TPS54302 VOUT → +5 V breadboard top rail
+5 V → SIM800L VCC (high-current branch)
+5 V → TPS7A0533 VIN
TPS7A0533 VOUT → +3.3 V breadboard bottom rail
+3.3 V → MSP430 VCC, NEO-6M VCC, TLV9001 V+

Signal connections

MSP430 P3.4 TX → NEO-6M RX
MSP430 P3.5 RX ← NEO-6M TX
MSP430 P2.5 TX → SIM800L RX
MSP430 P2.6 RX ← SIM800L TX
MSP430 P1.0 → 330Ω → status LED → GND
Panic button → TLV9001 buffer → MSP430 P1.1
SMA pigtail → NEO-6M ANT pad

All grounds tied to common GND rail. 100 nF ceramic + 10 µF electrolytic decoupling caps placed within 5 mm of each IC's VCC pin.
