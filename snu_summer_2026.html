Visa Requirements for Indian Students

C-3-9 Short-Term
🚨
India does NOT have visa-free access to South Korea. You need a C-3-9 (Short-Term General) visa. Processing takes 10–12 business days (standard) or 5–7 days (express). SNU recommends applying up to 1 month in advance. Apply immediately after receiving your SNU acceptance letter.

Tips & Practical Advice

India → Seoul
💸

Money in Seoul

Get a Niyo Global or Wise card — zero forex markup. Load KRW before leaving India or withdraw from Seoul ATMs. Cash is still widely used. Never exchange at airports.

🚇

Getting Around

Pick up a T-money card at any convenience store (GS25, CU, 7-Eleven) on arrival. Works on all subway, bus, and some taxis. Single ride: 1,400 KRW. Seoul Metro is clean, fast, and covers everywhere.

✈️

Flights

Book 3–4 months early — June/July is peak season from India. Air India has direct BOM–ICN and DEL–ICN. Korean Air and Asiana are premium alternatives. Use Google Flights price tracker.

🍱

Food

Campus cafeteria meals cost 4,500–6,000 KRW (~₹270–₹360). Many vegetarian options are limited in Korean cuisine — convenience store triangle kimbap (~₹60) is a lifesaver. GS25 and CU have great cheap meals.

📱

SIM Card

Buy at Incheon Airport arrivals hall immediately — KT, SKT, LG U+ all sell unlimited data SIMs. ~25,000–35,000 KRW for 30 days. Far cheaper and better than Indian roaming.

🏥

Health Insurance

SNU requires it. Buy a student travel insurance from Bajaj Allianz / HDFC Ergo before leaving. Get the full PDF policy — you'll need to upload it to the SNU portal and present it at dorm check-in.

📋

Visa Tip

Apply for the C-3-9 visa as soon as you get the SNU acceptance letter. BLS Mumbai/Hyderabad or VFS Delhi — book the appointment slot in advance online. Bring all originals + self-attested copies.

🗺️

Seoul Must-Dos (Free)

Gyeongbokgung Palace (free Sundays in hanbok), Han River parks, Bukchon Hanok Village, Namsan Seoul Tower (hike up free), Hongdae street art, Insadong market.

🌡️

Weather in June–July

Seoul in late June/July is hot and humid (28–34°C) with monsoon rains. Pack light summer clothes, a compact umbrella, and sunscreen. Dorm has AC.

🔌

Power Adapters

Korea uses Type C/F plugs, 220V. Indian plugs don't fit directly. Bring a universal adapter or buy one cheaply at Daiso in Seoul (₹100 equivalent).

📚

Course Credits

Confirm with IITB Academic Office whether SNU ISP credits are transferable before enrolling. Get it in writing. This varies by program and professor.

🤝

Networking

SNU ISP attracts students from 60+ countries. Go to orientation, join WhatsApp/KakaoTalk groups. The connections you make are often as valuable as the coursework.
