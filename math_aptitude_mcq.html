Conditional Probability
Q1
In a class of 100 students, 60 play cricket, 50 play football, and 30 play both. If a student is selected at random and found to play football, what is the probability that he also plays cricket?
  • (A) 0.50
  • (B) 0.30
  • (C) 0.60
  • (D) 0.18
Answer: (C) 0.60
P(C | F) = P(C ∩ F) / P(F) = (30/100) / (50/100) = 30/50 = 0.60.

Bayes' Theorem
Q2
In a binary communication channel, P(sending 1) = 0.8, P(receiving 1 | sent 1) = 0.95, and P(receiving 0 | sent 0) = 0.99. What is P(sent 1 | received 1)?
  • (A) 0.9973
  • (B) 0.9337
  • (C) 0.7993
  • (D) 0.8997
Answer: (A) 0.9973
P(rec 1 | sent 0) = 1 - 0.99 = 0.01. P(sent 0) = 0.2.
P(rec 1) = 0.8 × 0.95 + 0.2 × 0.01 = 0.76 + 0.002 = 0.762.
By Bayes': P(sent 1 | rec 1) = (0.8 × 0.95) / 0.762 = 0.76 / 0.762 ≈ 0.9974.
Source: GeeksforGeeks GATE Conditional Probability Quiz.
Bayes' Theorem
Q3
A disease affects 1 in 1000 people. A test is 99% accurate (both sensitivity and specificity). If a person tests positive, what is the approximate probability they actually have the disease?
  • (A) 0.99
  • (B) 0.09
  • (C) 0.50
  • (D) 0.01
Answer: (B) ~0.09 (about 9%)
P(D) = 0.001, P(+|D) = 0.99, P(+|D') = 0.01.
P(+) = 0.001 × 0.99 + 0.999 × 0.01 = 0.00099 + 0.00999 = 0.01098.
P(D|+) = 0.00099 / 0.01098 ≈ 0.0902 ~ 9%.
Classic illustration of base rate fallacy in Bayes' theorem.
Derangements
Q4
Five gentlemen check their hats at the door of a party. On leaving, each randomly picks a hat. In how many ways can this happen so that no one gets their own hat?
  • (A) 24
  • (B) 53
  • (C) 44
  • (D) 60
Answer: (C) 44
The number of derangements D(n) = n! ∑(k=0 to n) (-1)^k / k!
D(5) = 5! (1 - 1 + 1/2 - 1/6 + 1/24 - 1/120) = 120 × (44/120) = 44.

Pigeonhole Principle
Q5
A drawer contains 12 red socks and 12 blue socks. Socks are drawn at random in the dark. What is the minimum number of socks you must draw to guarantee having at least 2 blue socks?
  • (A) 4
  • (B) 14
  • (C) 13
  • (D) 3
Answer: (B) 14
Worst case: you draw all 12 red socks first. After that, the next 2 draws are guaranteed blue. So 12 + 2 = 14.
This is a direct application of the Pigeonhole Principle (worst-case guarantee).

Stars and Bars
Q6
How many non-negative integer solutions does x1 + x2 + x3 + x4 = 10 have?
  • (A) 120
  • (B) 220
  • (C) 330
  • (D) 286
Answer: (D) 286
By Stars and Bars, the number of non-negative integer solutions is C(n + k - 1, k - 1) = C(10 + 4 - 1, 4 - 1) = C(13, 3).
C(13,3) = 13! / (3! × 10!) = (13 × 12 × 11) / 6 = 286.

Inclusion-Exclusion
Q7
How many integers from 1 to 1000 are divisible by 3 or 5 or 7?
  • (A) 514
  • (B) 543
  • (C) 571
  • (D) 500
Answer: (B) 543
|A∪B∪C| = |A|+|B|+|C| - |A∩B| - |A∩C| - |B∩C| + |A∩B∩C|
⌊1000/3⌋ + ⌊1000/5⌋ + ⌊1000/7⌋ - ⌊1000/15⌋ - ⌊1000/21⌋ - ⌊1000/35⌋ + ⌊1000/105⌋
= 333 + 200 + 142 - 66 - 47 - 28 + 9 = 543.
Classic Inclusion-Exclusion Principle application.
Modular Arithmetic
Q8
What is the remainder when 72022 + 32022 is divided by 5?
  • (A) 0
  • (B) 2
  • (C) 3
  • (D) 4
Answer: (C) 3
7 ≡ 2 (mod 5), so 72022 ≡ 22022 (mod 5). Since 24 ≡ 1 (mod 5), and 2022 = 4×505 + 2, we get 22 = 4.
34 ≡ 1 (mod 5), and 2022 = 4×505 + 2, so 32022 ≡ 32 = 9 ≡ 4 (mod 5).
Total: 4 + 4 = 8 ≡ 3 (mod 5).

Rank of Matrix
Q14
If A is a 3×5 matrix with rank(A) = 3 and B is a 5×7 matrix with rank(B) = 5, what is rank(AB)?
  • (A) 3
  • (B) 5
  • (C) 7
  • (D) 2
Answer: (A) 3
By the rank inequality: rank(AB) ≤ min(rank(A), rank(B)) = min(3, 5) = 3.
Since B has full column rank relative to A's columns (rank(B) = 5 ≥ 5), and rank(A) = 3, the product AB has rank exactly 3.

Matrix Properties
Q15
Which of the following is always true for a real symmetric matrix?
  • (A) All eigenvalues are real
  • (B) All eigenvalues are positive
  • (C) The determinant is always positive
  • (D) The rank equals the number of rows
Answer: (A) All eigenvalues are real
The Spectral Theorem guarantees that a real symmetric matrix has only real eigenvalues and can be orthogonally diagonalized. Eigenvalues need not be positive (that requires positive definiteness), the determinant can be negative or zero, and the rank can be less than the number of rows if the matrix is singular.

Geometric Probability
Q16
A point is chosen uniformly at random inside a circle of radius R. What is the probability that the point is closer to the center than to the circumference?
  • (A) 1/4
  • (B) 1/2
  • (C) 1/3
  • (D) 3/4
Answer: (A) 1/4
A point at distance r from center is closer to center than to the edge when r < R - r, i.e., r < R/2.
The favorable area is a circle of radius R/2: π(R/2)2 = πR2/4.
Probability = (πR2/4) / (πR2) = 1/4.
Source: Sanfoundry Geometric Probability.
Geometric Probability
Q17
R is a random real number chosen uniformly between 5 and 9. What is the probability that R is closer to 5 than to 6?
  • (A) 1/4
  • (B) 1/8
  • (C) 1/2
  • (D) 3/8
Answer: (B) 1/8
R is closer to 5 than to 6 when R < 5.5 (the midpoint of 5 and 6).
Favorable interval: [5, 5.5), length = 0.5.
Total interval: [5, 9], length = 4.
Probability = 0.5 / 4 = 1/8 = 0.125.

Profit & Loss
Q18
A shopkeeper sells two items at the same price of Rs. 1000 each. On one he makes a 10% profit and on the other a 10% loss. What is the overall result of the transaction?
  • (A) 2% profit
  • (B) No profit, no loss
  • (C) 10% loss
  • (D) 1% loss
Answer: (D) 1% loss
Item 1 CP = 1000/1.10 = 909.09. Item 2 CP = 1000/0.90 = 1111.11.
Total CP = 2020.20. Total SP = 2000. Loss = 20.20.
Loss % = (20.20/2020.20) × 100 ≈ 1%.
Shortcut: When SP is same and profit% = loss% = x, there is always a loss of x2/100 = 100/100 = 1%.
Source: PlacementPreparation.io Profit & Loss MCQs.

  • (A) 5%
  • (B) 11%
  • (C) 5 5/19 %
  • (D) 4 4/19 %
Answer: (C) 5 5/19 %
Gain% = (Error / (True weight - Error)) × 100 = (50 / (1000 - 50)) × 100 = (50/950) × 100 = 100/19 = 5 5/19 %.

Combinatorics
Q20
From a class of 30 students (15 boys, 15 girls), 5 boys and 4 girls earned an A grade. A student is chosen at random. What is the probability that the student is a girl OR received an A grade?
  • (A) 1/4
  • (B) 3/10
  • (C) 1/3
  • (D) 2/3
Answer: (D) 2/3
By Inclusion-Exclusion: P(G ∪ A) = P(G) + P(A) - P(G ∩ A).
P(G) = 15/30 = 1/2. P(A) = 9/30 = 3/10. P(G ∩ A) = 4/30 = 2/15.
P(G ∪ A) = 1/2 + 3/10 - 2/15 = 15/30 + 9/30 - 4/30 = 20/30 = 2/3.
Source: GeeksforGeeks Probability Aptitude.
